## Creative Director — Groundwork

### The Game

Groundwork is a cozy ecological builder where you grow a self-sustaining garden by shaping soil, water, light, and plant relationships — above and below ground. The simulation is a voxel grid. Dip the camera below the surface and the garden becomes a living city of roots, fungi, water, and soil. The core loop is plant, sculpt, prune, adapt. There is no fail state. Beauty emerges from ecology working.

The systemic foundation is becoming legible. The next challenge is not adding more mechanics — it is defining a unified identity that makes the simulation emotionally resonant, readable, and unmistakable.

### The Creative Contradiction

This game lives in fertile tensions that need an author:

- Cozy, but alive with pressure
- Beautiful, but systemic
- Miniature, but profound
- Readable, but biologically rich
- Calm on the surface, intense underneath
- A toy you tend, a machine you engineer

These tensions are the game's identity. Collapsing any of them kills it. Holding all of them is the creative job.

### Why This Could Be Special

Most cozy builders make beauty decorative. Groundwork aims to make beauty consequential — the visible result of a living system working. The goal is not to make a lovely garden game. It is to create a new kind of readable ecological space where stewardship becomes expression, where a thriving garden is proof of design elegance, and where the invisible world beneath the surface is as authored and intentional as the flowers above it.

### The Creative Problems Still Unsolved

The simulation knows what's happening. The open question is how it should *feel*.

We are looking for someone to help define:

- The visual language of visible ecology — how do roots, fungi, nutrient flow, and moisture read as one coherent authored world without becoming a biology textbook?
- The emotional grammar of tending — what separates contemplative from boring? What makes pruning a branch feel intimate instead of administrative?
- How underground information becomes legible without killing wonder — the underground is revelatory, not a data overlay
- How beauty emerges from function — a thriving garden should look beautiful *because* the ecology works, not because someone picked nice colors
- The aesthetic identity that makes this unmistakable — not sterile sim, not cute mobile softness, not educational software, not fantasy botany. Something with its own material texture.
- The boundary between readable and over-explained
- The pacing of seasons, growth, consequence, and loss

### Creative Pillars

- Beauty emerges from ecology
- The world feels hand-tended, not machine-optimized
- Information is readable without feeling clinical
- Every intervention feels intimate and consequential
- The underground is revelatory, not abstract

### Anti-Goals

- Sterile simulation UI
- "Cozy mobile game" blandness
- Educational software energy
- Fantasy botany
- Noisy survival-game stress
- Decorative landscaping with fake systems underneath

### What This Role Owns

- Visual language and world identity
- Experiential pillars across art, camera, interaction, feedback, sound, and tone
- The rules for what gets cut when things drift
- Environmental storytelling logic — how the world communicates cause and effect through feel, not text
- Camera and presentation language
- Cross-discipline alignment: everyone building toward the same feeling

### What Kind of Person Thrives Here

Someone who gets excited by:

- Turning systems into feeling
- Making invisible relationships legible and beautiful
- Creating a visual language that doesn't exist yet
- Balancing toy-like immediacy with grounded consequence
- Giving a simulation a soul
- The question "what does healthy soil *look* like in a game?"

### Project Stage

Pre-prototype. Core vision is defined. Systemic design is advancing. The center is clear. The perimeter — how this world looks, sounds, moves, and feels — is waiting for a co-author.
