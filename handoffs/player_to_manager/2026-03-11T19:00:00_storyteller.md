# Player → Manager: Storyteller Session

**Date:** 2026-03-11T19:00:00
**Persona:** The Storyteller
**Session:** Tick 0–120, CLI play, 5 seeds planted, 3 rooted, 2 accidentally drowned

## Top Issues (by impact on player experience)

### 1. `fill` silently destroys seeds/roots — MAJOR
Used `fill water` to rescue a dying seed and it overwrote the seed instead. This was the session's defining tragedy and it's a genuine usability bug. `place` warns on overwrite; `fill` should too (or skip non-terrain materials).

### 2. ASCII water display misleads about growth viability — MODERATE
Map shows `~` next to seeds but water_level is below 30 (growth threshold). Player sees water, expects growth, gets "dormant — no water nearby." The visual and the simulation disagree. Either raise display threshold or lower growth threshold.

### 3. Root is an endpoint, not a lifecycle stage — MODERATE (design)
Once a seed becomes `*` root, nothing further happens. The story just stops. For the storyteller persona this kills replayability. Even minimal post-root behavior (spreading, water absorption, seed production) would create ongoing narrative.

### 4. Tick summaries don't name events — MINOR (high storyteller value)
"root: +1, seed: -1" doesn't say WHICH seed rooted. Named events in tick output ("Seed at (27,30) took root!") would be a small change with huge narrative payoff.

## What Worked Well

- **Inspect diagnostics are excellent for storytelling.** "dormant — no water nearby" reads like a story, not a spreadsheet. The growth progress bar and ticks-to-root prediction create genuine suspense.
- **s → S → * visual progression** is satisfying and readable.
- **Water expansion** from the central spring feels organic and alive.
- **The garden generated a real emotional arc** (hope → care → tragedy → acceptance) despite being text-only.
