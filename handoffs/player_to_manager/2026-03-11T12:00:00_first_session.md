# Player → Manager Handoff: First Play Session

**Source:** `feedback/2026-03-11T12:00:00_first_play_session.md`
**Build:** groundwork-sim + groundwork-tui (CLI), tick 0–220

---

## 1. Observed

- World creates successfully with stone base, soil middle layers, air above, and a 4x4 water spring at center surface (Z=16).
- Water spreads laterally and downward over ticks. Gravity flow works (water fills vertical shafts).
- Soil adjacent to water becomes wet soil (`%`), visually distinct from dry soil (`#`).
- Placing air into soil creates channels; water flows through them.
- Root material can be placed but has no behavior — no growth, no water absorption, no change over time.
- Light levels underground (Z=14) are high (~154) despite being surrounded by solid material.
- Nutrient levels are 0 everywhere.
- Out-of-bounds placement is rejected (with a raw Rust error message).
- Second water source spreads independently — multiple water systems coexist.

## 2. Felt

- Water flow is legible and satisfying. Digging a channel and watching it fill was the highlight.
- The flat uniform terrain is visually boring — no reason to explore the map.
- After experimenting with water routing, I had nothing left to try. No growth, no ecology, no payoff.
- The game currently feels like a water physics sandbox, not a garden builder.
- Placing a root and seeing it do nothing was deflating — it set up an expectation and broke it.

## 3. Bugs

1. **Light underground too high (major):** Z=14 voxels surrounded by soil show light_level ~154. Should attenuate through solids. Blocks meaningful plant growth logic.
2. **Water frontier has dotted edge (minor):** Alternating `~`/`.` pattern at expanding water boundary. Reads as a glitch.
3. **Raw Rust error for out-of-bounds (minor):** Error message not player-friendly.

## 4. Confusions

- Z-level labels ("above +1") don't clearly communicate relationship to surface.
- Placing soil over water replaces the water material but keeps water_level=255. Unclear what this means for gameplay.
- No indication of what root material does or is supposed to do.
- No help text explaining materials or gameplay systems.

## 5. What made me want to keep playing

- Watching water spread from the spring after first ticks — immediate, clear feedback.
- Digging a channel and seeing it fill — terrain sculpting has real potential.
- Imagining connecting two water sources with a canal.
- Seeing wet soil patterns and inferring water influence radius.

## 6. What made me want to stop

- Nothing grows. No seeds, no plants, no ecology.
- Flat uniform terrain with no features to discover or work around.
- CLI verbosity (every command is `cargo run -p groundwork-tui -- ...`).
- Placing voxels one at a time is too tedious for meaningful sculpting.
- After 15 minutes of water experiments, I ran out of things to do.

## 7. Requests

1. **(P0) One plantable seed species** — anything that grows in response to water/light. This is the missing core loop.
2. **(P1) Fix light attenuation underground** — prerequisite for plant growth to mean anything.
3. **(P1) Varied default terrain** — hills, depressions, exposed stone. Give the player something to work with.
4. **(P2) Batch voxel placement** — range syntax for sculpting (e.g., `place air 20..40 30 15`).
5. **(P2) Shorter CLI invocation** — alias or binary name so play sessions have less friction.
6. **(P2) Water depth visual** — distinguish shallow from deep water in ASCII.
7. **(P2) Clearer Z-level labels** — "1 above surface" instead of "above +1".
