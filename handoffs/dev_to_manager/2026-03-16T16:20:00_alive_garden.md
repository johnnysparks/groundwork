**Date:** 2026-03-16T16:20:00

## What Shipped

9 sprints focused entirely on "make the garden more alive":

1. **Denser canopy** — trees now branch in 8 directions (was 4) with 15 nodes/tick (was 10). Canopy fills in broader and faster.

2. **Visible squirrels** — squirrels were fully functional in the sim (caching acorns that sprout into oaks) but invisible in the web renderer. Now they have a warm chestnut 3D model with bushy tail, scurry/dig animations, and glow halo.

3. **Ecological event messages** — HUD now narrates ecological interactions as they happen: "A squirrel is burying an acorn — an oak may sprout here later!" and "A pollinator is visiting a flower — nearby plants get a health boost"

4. **Dusk fireflies** — 40 golden-green fireflies drift near ground level during golden hour and blue hour. They blink naturally (1-3s on, 0.5-2s off) with additive glow.

5. **Falling leaves** — 20 leaf particles constantly drift from canopy to ground with gentle swaying. Wind-responsive: faster/sideways in rain, slow in drought.

6. **Night crickets** — procedural dual-cricket chirp chorus fades in at dusk. Extremely quiet (0.03 volume) — barely audible background texture.

7. **Nitrogen handshake visual** — green ecology particles rise from ground near trees with nearby groundcover. Makes the 1.5x nitrogen boost visually discoverable.

8. **Plant hover tooltip** — hovering over any plant voxel shows species name and material type. Helps the discovery arc.

9. **Wind-responsive leaves** — falling leaves respond to weather wind strength, connecting weather to ambient particles.

## What Needs Playtesting

- **Fireflies + crickets at dusk** — these only activate during golden hour (day cycle time 0.65+). The automated screenshots start at noon (0.5) so they're not captured. Manual play or a dusk-timed screenshot would show them.
- **Nitrogen shimmer density** — the green particles might be too prominent or too subtle at default zoom. Needs player eyes.
- **Squirrel visibility at default zoom** — squirrels are 3.0 scale but on the ground; they might be hard to spot in a busy garden.
- **Ecological event message frequency** — cooldowns are set to 40+ ticks; might be too rare or too frequent.
- **Tooltip responsiveness** — raycasting every mousemove might be heavy on low-end devices.
