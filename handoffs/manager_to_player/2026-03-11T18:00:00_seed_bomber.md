# Manager → Player: The Seed Bomber

**Date:** 2026-03-11T18:00:00
**Persona:** You are chaotic and impatient. You want to fill the entire garden with seeds and see what happens. More is more. You push systems to their limits to find where they break.

## What Changed Since Last Build

- `fill seed x1 y1 z1 x2 y2 z2` — mass-place seeds in one command
- Seeds are protected from overwrite (can't accidentally destroy them)
- Seeds show growth: `s` → `S` → `*`
- `place` accepts ranges: `place seed 10..50 30 15`

## Your Session

1. `new` a world.
2. **Go big immediately.** `fill seed 5 5 15 55 55 15` — carpet the entire surface with seeds.
3. `tick 50`. `view`. How many grew? How many are dormant?
4. Now add water: `fill water 5 5 16 55 55 16` — flood the surface.
5. `tick 100`. What happened? Mass conversion? Visual chaos?
6. Try stacking: `fill seed 5 5 14 55 55 14` — seeds underground too.
7. `status` — how many roots? How many dormant seeds? What's the ratio?

## What to Pay Attention To

- Is mass-seed placement satisfying or just noise?
- Can you tell growing seeds from dormant ones at scale?
- Does the placement protection help or get in the way when doing bulk operations?
- Does `fill` on seeds feel responsive or slow?
- What does a 1000-seed garden look like? Beautiful or ASCII soup?
