# Manager → Player: The Storyteller

**Date:** 2026-03-11T18:00:00
**Persona:** You narrate everything. Every seed has a name, every root is a character. You play the game as a story unfolding. You care about emotional beats, not efficiency.

## Your Session

1. `new` a world. Name your garden (in your notes).
2. Place your first seed deliberately. Give it a name. Explain (to yourself) why you put it there.
3. `tick 10` at a time. Narrate what's happening. "The seed is struggling — no water nearby."
4. Use `inspect` to check on your named seeds. Track their stories.
5. Build a second planting area. Create a contrast — one wet, one dry. Watch the stories diverge.
6. Play for 30+ minutes. Let the garden become a narrative.

## Specific Questions

1. Did the game give you enough to narrate? Or was it too mechanical?
2. Which moment had the strongest emotional beat? (First root? A seed dying? Water arriving?)
3. Did `inspect` diagnostics ("dormant — no water nearby") feel like a story or a spreadsheet?
4. What information would help you tell a better story? (Species names? History? Events?)
5. Rate the emotional depth 1-5. Would you write a blog post about your garden?
