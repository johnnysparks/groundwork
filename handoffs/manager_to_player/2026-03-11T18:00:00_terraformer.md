# Manager → Player: The Terraformer

**Date:** 2026-03-11T18:00:00
**Persona:** You are a landscape sculptor. You care about terrain shape first, plants second. Your garden bed is a canvas and `fill` is your brush.

## What Changed Since Last Build

- `fill <mat> x1 y1 z1 x2 y2 z2` — fill rectangular regions in one command
- `place` accepts ranges: `place soil 20..40 30 15` for rows
- Seeds/roots are protected from accidental overwrite (`--force` to override)
- Seeds show growth: `s` → `S` → `*`

## Your Session

1. `new` a world. `view` to see the flat terrain.
2. **Sculpt first, plant later.** Use `fill` to carve valleys, raise hills, create terraces, dig channels. Try to make the terrain interesting before any seeds go down.
3. Try building: a terraced hillside, a sunken bowl, a raised plateau with a moat, a zigzag water channel.
4. Once terrain is shaped, add water and seeds. Tick 50-100 times. See how your terrain shapes where growth happens.

## What to Pay Attention To

- Is `fill` intuitive? Did you get the coordinate order right on first try?
- Can you make terrain that looks *designed* in ASCII? Or does everything flatten to `#`?
- Does shaping terrain feel creative or mechanical?
- After adding water to your sculpted terrain — does it flow the way you expected?

## Specific Questions

1. What terrain shape did you build? Did it survive water/ticks intact?
2. Did `fill` ever do something unexpected? Wrong region, wrong axis?
3. What's missing from the terrain toolkit? (Don't hold back — but label wishes vs. needs.)
4. Rate the creative satisfaction of sculpting terrain 1-5.
5. After sculpting + planting, what did you want to do next?
