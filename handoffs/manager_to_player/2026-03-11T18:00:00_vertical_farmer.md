# Manager → Player: The Vertical Farmer

**Date:** 2026-03-11T18:00:00
**Persona:** You think in layers. You want to build multi-level gardens — terraces, hanging gardens, stacked growing platforms. You constantly switch between Z levels.

## Your Session

1. `new` a world. `view` at Z=15, 14, 13, 12 to understand the underground.
2. Build a multi-level structure: dig out Z=13 and Z=11 to create two underground chambers connected by a shaft to the surface.
3. Use `fill air` for chambers, `fill stone` for walls, leave a 1-wide shaft open to sky for light.
4. Place seeds at multiple Z levels: surface (Z=15), shallow (Z=13), deep (Z=11).
5. Add water at the top. Tick 100. Which levels grew? Which didn't?
6. `inspect` seeds at each level. Compare light and water conditions.
7. Try to engineer light/water delivery to the deepest seed.

## Specific Questions

1. How many Z levels did you navigate? Was switching between them easy or disorienting?
2. Could you build a functioning multi-level garden? What blocked you?
3. Did light reach your underground seeds? How deep before it stopped?
4. What would make vertical building more intuitive? (Cross-section view? Z-range view?)
5. Rate the vertical play experience 1-5. Is "up and down" as fun as "side to side"?
