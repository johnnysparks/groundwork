# Build Notes: Green Garden + Species Colors (Sprints 94-96)

**Date:** 2026-03-16T14:30:00
**Sprints:** 94-96

## What Changed

### Sprint 94: Green Carpet Groundcover
- Groundcover seeds planted in grid pattern (±25 from center, 8-voxel spacing) = ~49 seeds in watered zone
- Previous seeding: ±7 from center = ~15 seeds in a tiny cluster
- Dispersal distance doubled: 0.5m→1.0m (10→20 voxels) for all groundcover
- Pre-ticks increased 100→200 for generation-2 maturation
- **Key discovery**: seeds need `water_level >= 30` to germinate — only seeds within water reach (~25 voxels from spring) can grow. Grid seeding constrained to watered zone.

### Sprint 95: Greener Garden Floor (Rendering)
- Surface soil grass color: `(0.28, 0.40, 0.18)` → `(0.18, 0.52, 0.14)` — much more visibly green
- Grass zone extended: z >= GROUND_LEVEL-1 → z >= GROUND_LEVEL-6 (covers rolling terrain)
- Side faces at z >= GROUND_LEVEL-1 also get grass coloring (green lip effect)
- Garden floor now reads as grassy meadow instead of bare dirt

### Sprint 96: Species-Specific Voxel Leaf Colors
- Added `SPECIES_LEAF` color table to terrain.ts (12 species)
- Leaf voxels now use species_id (byte 3) for color, matching the billboard foliage system
- Canopy shows species diversity: oak=deep forest, birch=lime, pine=dark blue, wildflower=pink, daisy=gold
- Colors slightly muted vs billboard sprites so voxel mesh reads as solid structure

## Architecture Notes
- Terrain renderer (terrain.ts) handles voxel geometry with species-specific colors for ALL materials: Trunk, Branch, Root, Leaf
- Foliage renderer (foliage.ts) adds semi-transparent billboard sprites on top of every Leaf voxel
- The two systems complement each other: voxels for structure, sprites for lush soft overlay
- Grid voxel data: `[material: u8, water_level: u8, light_level: u8, species_id: u8]`

## Key Discovery: Water Reach Limits Groundcover
The biggest blocker for "green carpet" isn't dispersal rate or seeding density — it's that the water spring only reaches ~25 voxels from center in the garden, and seeds need water to germinate. Edges of the 120×120 garden will always be dry unless:
- The water spring is made stronger
- Rainfall events wet the whole surface
- A second water source is added
