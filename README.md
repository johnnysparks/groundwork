# GROUNDWORK GAME PITCH

**A cozy ecological builder where you grow a self-sustaining garden by shaping soil, water, light, and plant relationships — above and below ground.**

---

## The Fantasy

Build a living miniature world that becomes more beautiful, resilient, and alive every season.

You are not managing plants. You are composing an ecosystem. Every choice changes the conditions for what grows next. An oak matures and suddenly the sunny bed beneath it is a shade garden. Your groundcover self-seeds into the gap you left. A pest finds your monoculture. The birdbath you placed last spring brought wrens that eat the aphids before they spread.

Twelve seasons in, you haven't touched the garden in three. It's sustaining itself. You earned that.

---

## Core Play

**Plant.** Choose a species, place a seed. It grows based on where you put it — light, water, neighbors, soil.

**Sculpt.** Dig basins, build mounds, grade slopes. Water flows through the landscape you shape.

**Prune.** Cut, weed, deadhead, thin. Small frequent acts that redirect the garden's future.

**Irrigate.** Route water. Build structures — beehives, birdbaths, trellises — that shift ecological dynamics.

**Adapt.** Maturing plants change conditions for everything nearby. Neglect creates opportunistic spread. Success in one layer creates stress in another. Each season tests your design. The garden is always asking you to respond.

---

## The Underground

Dip the camera below the surface. Same world, same simulation, no mode switch.

Root networks branch like road infrastructure. Fungal threads pulse between connected plants. Water seeps through soil layers. Nutrient hotspots glow. Worms tunnel.

The surface shows beauty. The underground reveals why it works.

---

## Why It's Fun

Every plant changes the map. Every season reveals whether your design holds. Every success creates new problems.

**Minute 1:** Plant a seed. Watch it sprout.

**Minute 10:** Your third plant is outperforming your first two. You notice it gets more sun. You start thinking about placement.

**Hour 1:** Bees visit your flowers. Roots connect underground. You pull your first weed. You're reading the system.

**Hour 10:** A dense, layered, self-seeding web. You're sculpting terrain to redirect water. You're composing, not just planting. You want to try a new biome.

The hook: every plant you add changes the system. You want to see what happens next.

---

## Tension Without Punishment

No fail state. Your garden always looks like a garden. But there is pressure:

- Maturing trees reshape light for everything beneath them
- Neglected soil compacts and drains poorly
- Monocultures attract plagues; diversity resists them
- Invasive species spread if unchecked
- Every season is a test you didn't write

The difference between a novice garden and a master garden is vibrancy, density, resilience — not pass vs. fail.

---

## Expression

The garden you build reflects your taste:

- Wild native meadow buzzing with pollinators
- Shaded woodland retreat under layered canopy
- Formal structured bed with clean edges and seasonal color
- Drought-proof xeriscape — lean, efficient, alive

Not cosmetic choices. Ecological ones that produce different aesthetics as a consequence.

---

## MVP

**One biome.** Temperate. 12–20 species across three tiers (canopy, shrub, groundcover).

**Four systems:** Light, water, roots, ecology.

**One bed.** ~120×120×60 voxels (60m×60m×30m at 0.5m detail). Room for 2-3 trees, a handful of shrubs, a carpet of groundcover.

**One continuous camera** — above and below ground.

**Terrain sculpting. Seed placement. Basic tools.**

**One challenge:** make a bed self-sustaining through four seasons.

That is the game. Everything else is expansion.

---

## Progression

New species and tools unlock through play — not tutorials, but milestones that emerge naturally. A canopy tree reaching maturity opens shade-tolerant species. A thriving pollinator population unlocks rarer flowering plants. Reaching biodiversity thresholds opens new biomes: desert, tropical, alpine. Each brings new species, new constraints, new expression.

---

## Visual Direction

**Voxel simulation.** Terrain, water, roots, and growth all operate in one living grid. Compressed diorama scale: a seed is 1-2 voxels, a canopy tree is 20-30. Real ratio is 1:10,000; game ratio is 1:30. Everything legible at one zoom. Growth is visible at every stage — the voxels are the progress bar.

**Prototype:** Colored cubes. If the simulation is satisfying to watch as blocks, the game works.

**V1:** High-res voxel (Town to City quality). Seasonal palettes. Shape variation on plant voxels. Distinct underground visual treatment.

---

## Audience

**Built for** cozy-systems players **who love** Dorfromantik and Tiny Glade **but want** a living world that responds to their choices with real depth.

## What the Prototype Must Prove

Players can predictably improve a garden through visible ecological relationships, and watching those relationships unfold is satisfying enough to drive "one more seed" play.

If that's true, everything else follows.
