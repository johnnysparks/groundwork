# Feedback: Trees Snap Between Growth Stages

**Date:** 2026-03-16T10:17:00
**Source:** Live mobile playtest (johnnysparks.github.io)
**Severity:** Core feel — breaks the cozy gardening fantasy

## The Problem

Trees don't grow a voxel at a time. They SNAP between discrete growth stages (Seedling → Sapling → YoungTree → Mature → OldGrowth). One tick the tree is a tiny seedling, the next tick it's a full sapling with trunk and canopy. There's no gradual expansion.

## Why This Matters

The game's core fantasy is watching things grow. "Watch it grow" is literally a quest objective. But the growth is invisible — you see a seed, then suddenly a tree appears. The satisfying moment of seeing a trunk extend one voxel at a time, a canopy slowly filling in — that doesn't exist.

## What Smooth Growth Looks Like

- Trunk extends upward one voxel per few ticks
- Canopy adds leaf voxels gradually around branch tips
- Roots visibly spread underground tick by tick
- The player can WATCH the growth happen, not just see the result

## Root Cause

`tree_rasterize` in systems.rs clears the entire footprint and re-writes the full template/skeleton on every dirty flag. Growth stages set `dirty = true` which triggers a complete re-rasterize. Between stages, the tree looks static.

## Fix Direction

Instead of re-rasterizing the entire tree on stage transition, accumulate voxels gradually:
- Each tick, add 1-3 new voxels to the tree's footprint
- Trunk grows upward, branches extend outward, leaves fill canopy
- Stage transitions just change growth parameters, not the visual all at once
