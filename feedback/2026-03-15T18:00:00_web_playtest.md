# Feedback: Web Game Playtest — Visual Experience & Simulation via WASM

**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Build:** Post-fauna, post-foliage renderer, WASM bridge connected
**Session:** ~560 ticks via Playwright + agentAPI, 14 screenshots captured across 7 phases
**Focus:** Web visual experience, WASM integration, growth progression, garden feel

---

## 1. What the game sold me

A cozy 3D voxel garden where I shape terrain, plant seeds, and watch an interconnected ecosystem come alive. Warm golden lighting, visible fauna, species interactions, and a self-sustaining world that surprises me.

## 2. What I actually experienced

The visual foundation is genuinely beautiful. The warm amber/ochre soil palette, the isometric cross-section view showing underground layers, the subtle contour-line texture on the terrain surface, and the golden-hour lighting all deliver "cozy" immediately. The HUD is clean and the species picker is well-organized by plant type (Tree/Shrub/Flower/Ground). The quest panel on the left gives clear onboarding instructions.

Growth is visible but **tiny and fragile-looking.** A single tree emerges at the center of the terrain after 150+ ticks — a thin trunk with a small cluster of green leaf voxels. At 350+ ticks, a second growth area appears. By 550 ticks the garden has two small plants and one larger tree, with 1083 leaves, 47 trunks, and 384 deadwood. The garden looks sparse and lonely on the vast 80x80 terrain.

The water rendering is lovely — a translucent blue-white surface that sits naturally on the terrain. The water stream/channel effect visible in the side views is charming.

## 3. Best moments

- **First tree emerging (tick ~150).** Watching a small green canopy appear above the trunk is the core fantasy starting to deliver. The foliage renderer draws the leaves as slightly transparent billboards with a soft green glow — it looks alive.
- **The terrain cross-section.** The isometric cutaway showing soil layers, underground water, and the terrain contours is beautiful and informative. You can see "inside" the garden — a unique visual identity.
- **Water rendering.** The translucent water surface catches light convincingly. The stream/channel visible from the close-up view at tick 350 looks natural.
- **Low-angle view at tick 350.** The tree silhouette against the warm amber terrain with green leaf clusters looks like a real miniature world. This is the "postcard moment" — the game's visual identity crystallized.
- **Top-down view (05-mid-growth-topdown).** This is the best screenshot — the HUD is fully legible, you can see the entire garden bed, the species picker labels are all readable, and the isometric terrain with the emerging tree looks like a game people would want to play.

## 4. Surprises — things the garden did that I didn't plan

- **Deadwood appeared (384 voxels by tick 350).** Some plants died and left deadwood behind — the first sign that the garden has its own lifecycle, not just growth. This is ecologically meaningful (decomposition).
- **Seed count increased from 13 → 19** between tick 350 and 550. Seed dispersal is working — the garden is autonomously planting. I didn't place those extra 6 seeds.
- **Water count decreased from 357 → 183** as the garden grew. Plants are drinking the water I placed — a visible resource competition dynamic.

## 5. Confusing moments

- **Seeds planted at z=50 are invisible.** I placed 13 seeds but the screenshots at tick 10 look identical to tick 0. No visual feedback that seeds were placed. Seeds need a particle effect, a small sprite, or some indicator.
- **Growth stall between early growth and mid growth.** At tick 60 (phase 4), materials show 0 roots, 0 trunks — nothing grew. By tick 160 (phase 5), growth finally appeared. A player would wonder if the game is broken during that 100-tick gap.
- **The "Tick" counter in the top-left stays at "Tick: 0" in all screenshots.** This is because the agentAPI ticks don't update the HUD display. But for a real player this would be confusing — no visible timer or growth progress indicator.
- **Quest log says "Move the camera" even at tick 550.** The quest system doesn't advance based on garden actions. It feels disconnected from what I'm actually doing.
- **Where's the water I placed?** I filled a 10x10 water region but it's hard to see on the terrain. The water rendering works, but the fill-tool feedback is subtle.

## 6. Boring or frustrating moments

- **Vast empty terrain.** 80x80 voxels is a huge canvas. After 550 ticks with 13 species planted, there are only 2-3 visible plant clusters. The garden feels empty and the terrain dominates. Either the growth needs to be faster/larger, or the initial garden should be smaller.
- **No visible fauna at any point.** The fauna system exists in the sim (FaunaList) but nothing appeared visually during 550 ticks. No bees, no birds, no worms. The garden feels lifeless — just plants and terrain.
- **Growth is slow relative to visual payoff.** 550 ticks for ~1000 leaves is underwhelming visually. The leaves are nice but small. Most of the screen is still bare terrain.

## 7. Signs of life — fauna, movement, autonomous garden behavior (or lack)

- **Fauna: None visible.** The FaunaRenderer exists in the codebase but no fauna appeared during the session. This is the biggest gap relative to the game's promise of "teeming with life."
- **Autonomous behavior: Minimal but present.** Seed dispersal (+6 seeds) and deadwood formation (384 voxels) show the garden has some agency. But the pace is glacial.
- **Movement: None.** Everything is static between ticks. No wind sway visible on foliage (the code exists but may not be triggering), no particle effects beyond the initial growth burst, no ambient motion.

## 8. What I learned about the ecosystem (or: what felt like there was nothing left to learn)

- Seeds need water nearby to germinate (learned from CLI testing — seeds at z=45 with 0 water stayed dormant indefinitely)
- Plants consume water over time (357 → 183 water voxels)
- Dead plants leave deadwood (384 voxels appeared without my action)
- Seed dispersal works autonomously (13 → 19 seeds)
- I did NOT learn about: species interactions, competition, nitrogen fixing, pollination, root wars, canopy effects. None of these were observable in this session.

## 10. Feature or clarity requests

1. **Seed placement indicator.** When a seed is placed, show a small sprite or particle burst so the player knows it worked.
2. **Growth progress bar or timer.** Some visual feedback that growth is happening between tick 0 and the first visible sprout.
3. **Fauna rendering.** Even particle-like dots drifting between flowers would make the garden feel alive. This is the single most impactful missing feature.
4. **Smaller starting garden OR faster growth.** The 80x80 terrain feels vast and empty. Either shrink the camera focus to a 30x30 area, or make growth 3-5x faster to fill the space.
5. **Species variety visual differentiation.** Currently all growth looks similar — green leaf clusters on brown trunks. Different tree species should look visibly different (birch = white trunk, pine = dark green, willow = drooping).
6. **Water tool feedback.** Show a brief blue splash or ripple when water is placed.
7. **Idle animation.** Even subtle leaf sway or water ripple would prevent the "screensaver" feeling during idle time.

## 11. Brutal bottom line: would I come back tomorrow — and *why*?

**Probably not yet, but I'm close to wanting to.**

The visual identity is strong — the warm amber terrain with green foliage, the isometric cross-section, and the golden lighting all feel genuinely cozy and inviting. The foundation of a beautiful game is here. But:

- The garden doesn't feel **alive.** No fauna, no movement, no ambient life. It feels like a still image that very slowly changes.
- I didn't discover **any interactions.** I planted clover near oak for nitrogen fixing, wildflowers for pollinators, berry-bush for birds — and none of those relationships materialized visually or in the data. The ecological fantasy is entirely theoretical.
- The **payoff is too slow** for the visual promise. After 550 ticks the garden is still mostly empty terrain with a couple of small plants. The first-impression hook works (the terrain is gorgeous), but then nothing happens for too long.

**What would bring me back:**
- Seeing a bee drift from my wildflower to my daisy
- Watching the oak grow noticeably faster because I planted clover nearby
- Having a bird carry a seed from my berry-bush to a spot across the garden
- Any one visible, traceable interaction chain

The visual renderer is ready. The simulation has the systems. The missing piece is **making those systems visible at human-watchable timescales.**
