## What the game looks like now

**Production quality.** The hero shot at 2,996 with warm atmospheric fog, organic foliage clusters, vignette framing, and bloom glow on canopy edges — this looks like a published indie game. The blue hour shot (3,859) is genuinely beautiful and shareable.

## Best moments

- **Blue hour garden** — golden foliage glowing against dark sky. This is the game's signature visual.
- **Close-up with tilt-shift** — miniature photography feel. You can see the gnome figure among the trees.
- **Score trend "+"** — constant visual feedback that the garden is growing.
- **Foliage clustering** — canopies look organic, not like individual dots.
- **Day cycle auto-cycling** — lighting shifts naturally during play.

## What's working

- All visual elements share the warm earthy palette
- Competition creates visible spacing between trees
- Health stress shows as subtle amber tinting (not overwhelming)
- Event feed narrates ecology in real-time
- Gnome visible with task queue status

## What's still needed

1. **The garden is still mostly amber** — even at noon, foliage tends golden. Need more species-specific green variety.
2. **Gnome is hard to see** — the procedural shape is small among the canopy. The style team's SDF gnome may help.
3. **No sound** — the visual beauty wants audio. Water, wind, birds.
4. **Drag-to-zone** — single click works but drag painting would feel better.
